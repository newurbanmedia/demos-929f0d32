Foundations

1.1Color

Magenta is the primary voice: brand identity, primary CTAs, links, focus rings, active states. Blue is a strategic accent used sparingly, at most once per visible section, for info-state callouts and moments that need a second note of emphasis. They sit far enough apart on the color wheel to read as intentional rather than competing.

Accessibility notes

Magenta #D80052 achieves 5.2:1 contrast against white, passing WCAG AA for all text. Blue #0B7FBD achieves 5.0:1. Text links on white use magenta-700 #880035 at 10.4:1 for AAA contrast. Verify every text color against its rendered background during QA using Stark or axe.

✓ Do

  • Use Poppins for every heading from H1 through H5 across the entire site.
  • Use Fraunces only in pull quote body copy. Nowhere else.
  • Use JetBrains Mono for tokens, category labels, timestamps, form meta, stat labels, and any technical microcopy.
  • Use magenta for primary actions, links, focus rings, brand identity, and service pillar signatures.
  • Use blue strategically: at most one blue element per visible section, for info-state callouts, info badges, and moments that earn a second note.
  • Keep neumorphic depth on containers and passive surfaces. Use solid fills on interactive elements that carry meaning.
  • Maintain visible focus rings on every interactive element.

✕ Don't

  • Avoid Fraunces in any heading, body paragraph, button, form, caption, or UI element. Pull quotes only.
  • Avoid JetBrains Mono for headings or body paragraphs. It's strictly technical metadata.
  • Avoid blue where magenta would also work. Blue is an accent, not a second primary.
  • Avoid multiple blue elements in the same visible section.
  • Avoid stacking neumorphic surfaces more than two layers deep.
  • Avoid removing focus rings. They're required, not decorative.
  • Avoid oversize headlines. Page titles cap at ~44px, hero H1 at ~40px. Heading hierarchy communicates, size alone doesn't.
