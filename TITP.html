Overview

About This Campaign

Theatre in the Park is a free, bilingual outdoor theatre series produced by Cazateatro Bilingual Theatre Group, presented across four dates in the fall of 2026: September 12 & 19 and October 3 & 10. Performances are free and open to the public, reflecting Cazateatro's mission to bring bilingual theatre to the entire Memphis community.


Two poster designs have been developed for this campaign: a General Event Poster to promote the series as a whole, and a Production-Specific Poster that introduces the featured work, The Ant & Mr. Q. Both designs share a unified visual identity and can be deployed simultaneously across print and digital channels.

Pending information: The name and address of the park venue have not yet been confirmed by Cazateatro. This information must be added to both poster files before final production and distribution.

Version A — General Event Poster

This poster serves as the primary campaign visual for the Theatre in the Park series. It introduces the event without referencing a specific production, making it suitable for early-season promotion, general awareness, and contexts where a single announcement will cover all four dates. The central illustration — a large silhouetted female figure in profile, surrounded by stylized trees, architectural line drawings, and a diverse audience seated on the grass — communicates the outdoor, community-centered spirit of the event. Warm, saturated greens and golds anchor the color palette against a sunlit yellow background.

Version B — Production Poster: The Ant & Mr. Q

This version builds directly on the general poster's visual identity while introducing the featured production: The Ant & Mr. Q. The most significant visual modification — agreed upon in the design review meeting with the Cazateatro team — is the addition of a wide-brimmed hat on the central figure, reinforcing the character identity of Mr. Q. A line of animated ants marching across the stage illustration has also been incorporated into the scene, connecting the visual to the production's story world. The production title is set in a bold, wide sans-serif treatment at the bottom of the poster in a high-contrast green band, ensuring strong legibility at a distance and at reduced sizes.

Side by Side

Version Comparison

Both posters are designed to work as a system — deployed simultaneously at different stages of campaign promotion or across different channels.

Version A — General

  • No production title — promotes the series
  • Central figure: bare silhouette in profile
  • No production-specific illustration elements
  • Ideal for early promotion and general awareness
  • Evergreen across all four dates
  • Works for venues, sponsors, and partners without production context

Version B — Production

  • Introduces The Ant & Mr. Q as the featured work
  • Central figure wears wide-brimmed hat (per team review)
  • Ants illustrated on stage — production narrative tie-in
  • Production title anchors lower third in bold green band
  • For use once production is announced
  • Suitable for ticketing pages, event-specific social posts, and printed programs
